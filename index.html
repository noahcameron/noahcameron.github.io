Forest Fire Spread Model

Utilizing ArcPy, our model simulates the spread of a forest fire. Spread is simulated using a tessellation of hexagons.
The fire will start from one hexagon and based on a variety of factors, aggregated based on underlaying GIS data,
will determine a probability that the fire spreads to its neighbour (0%-100%). RNG is used to determine if the spread occurs
this continues recursively until spread stops naturally.

The weights of each factor (soil type, temperature, etc) were based on a MLR analysis on how factors impact the total area burned.

Tracking Oil Spills with Remote Sensing

Over the course of four-months our team of three researched existing methods for tracking oil spills
using remote sensing. We looked to improve on existing tracking methods by combining the use of three
sensors, and tested our methods by tracking a large oil spill in Kuwait.

The results are reviewed in the full research paper.
